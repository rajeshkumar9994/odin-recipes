1.Ven Pongal Recipe

Description

Ven Pongal is a popular South Indian breakfast dish made with rice, moong dal, ghee, black pepper, cumin, and cashew nuts. It is soft, flavorful, and usually served with sambar and coconut chutney

Ingredients

  • Rice: 1 cup (short- or medium-grain varieties like seeraga samba, sona masuri, ponni, or surti kolam)
  • Moong dal: ¼ to ½ cup (split yellow lentils, husked)
  • Ghee: 2-4 tablespoons (clarified butter)
  • Cumin seeds: 1-1½ teaspoons
  • Black peppercorns: 1 teaspoon whole or coarsely crushed
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, finely chopped or grated
  • Curry leaves: 1 sprig (10-12 leaves)
  • Cashews: 10-12, whole or halved
  • Asafoetida (Hing): ⅛ teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste
  • Water: 3-4 cups, adjusted for desired consistency

Steps how to make it:-

  1. Dry roast the moong dal for 23 minutes until aromatic.
  2. Wash the rice and roasted moong dal thoroughly
  3. Add the rice, dal, water, and salt to a pressure cooker.
  4. Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles until the mixture becomes soft and mushy.
  5. Open the cooker and mash the cooked rice and dal gently.
  6. Heat ghee in a small pan.
  7. Add black pepper and cumin seeds; allow them to crackle.
  8. Add chopped ginger, cashew nuts, curry leaves, and asafoetida.
  9. Fry until the cashews turn golden brown.
  10. Pour this tempering over the cooked pongal mixture
  11. Mix everything well. If the pongal is too thick, add a little hot water to adjust the consistency.
  12. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
